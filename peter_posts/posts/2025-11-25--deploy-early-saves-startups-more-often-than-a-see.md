🚀 𝐃𝐞𝐩𝐥𝐨𝐲 𝐞𝐚𝐫𝐥𝐲 𝐬𝐚𝐯𝐞𝐬 𝐬𝐭𝐚𝐫𝐭𝐮𝐩𝐬 𝐦𝐨𝐫𝐞 𝐨𝐟𝐭𝐞𝐧 𝐭𝐡𝐚𝐧 𝐚 𝐬𝐞𝐞𝐝 𝐫𝐨𝐮𝐧𝐝.

I have a friend who has been building his startup for three months already.
He showed me a demo on his laptop one and a half months ago — a real technical solution, everything looks nice, and he can demo it perfectly.
But when I ask:“𝘖𝘬𝘢𝘺, 𝘩𝘰𝘸 𝘤𝘢𝘯 𝘐 𝘵𝘦𝘴𝘵 𝘪𝘵? 𝘑𝘶𝘴𝘵 𝘵𝘳𝘺 𝘪𝘵 𝘮𝘺𝘴𝘦𝘭𝘧 𝘵𝘰 𝘶𝘯𝘥𝘦𝘳𝘴𝘵𝘢𝘯𝘥 𝘪𝘧 𝘪𝘵 𝘸𝘰𝘳𝘬𝘴?”— it turns out I cannot.
𝐓𝐡𝐞 𝐟𝐮𝐧𝐧𝐲 𝐭𝐡𝐢𝐧𝐠 𝐢𝐬, 𝐢𝐭 𝐨𝐧𝐥𝐲 𝐰𝐨𝐫𝐤𝐬 𝐨𝐧 𝐡𝐢𝐬 𝐥𝐚𝐩𝐭𝐨𝐩. 


And this is a super typical problem. 
It’s not only him — most engineers I know think and act like this. 
They focus so much on building the solution exactly how 𝘵𝘩𝘦𝘺 see it, that they forget one simple thing: 
it must be valuable for others, not only for them.
𝐈𝐟 𝐲𝐨𝐮 𝐜𝐚𝐧𝐧𝐨𝐭 𝐬𝐡𝐨𝐰 𝐢𝐭, 𝐲𝐨𝐮 𝐚𝐫𝐞 𝐧𝐨𝐭 𝐛𝐮𝐢𝐥𝐝𝐢𝐧𝐠 𝐚 𝐩𝐫𝐨𝐝𝐮𝐜𝐭. 
𝐘𝐨𝐮 𝐚𝐫𝐞 𝐣𝐮𝐬𝐭 𝐰𝐫𝐢𝐭𝐢𝐧𝐠 𝐬𝐨𝐦𝐞𝐭𝐡𝐢𝐧𝐠 𝐢𝐧 𝐭𝐡𝐞 𝐫𝐞𝐩𝐨𝐬𝐢𝐭𝐨𝐫𝐲.


Deploy early means one thing: 
💡 before you write code or build a feature, your result should already be something that can be shown to the world. 
Even if it’s just “Hello World.”


It sounds stupid, but it saves you. 
It forces you to focus not on the code, but on the user and the impact — even before you start building anything. It stops a technical founder from running away into code and disconnecting from reality.


A small step into production is always a huge step toward the right product.
𝐁𝐞𝐟𝐨𝐫𝐞 𝐭𝐡𝐢𝐧𝐤𝐢𝐧𝐠 “𝐰𝐞 𝐧𝐞𝐞𝐝 𝐭𝐨 𝐦𝐚𝐤𝐞 𝐢𝐭 𝐩𝐞𝐫𝐟𝐞𝐜𝐭,” 𝐚𝐬𝐤 𝐲𝐨𝐮𝐫𝐬𝐞𝐥𝐟: “𝐡𝐨𝐰 𝐰𝐢𝐥𝐥 𝐰𝐞 𝐬𝐡𝐨𝐰 𝐭𝐡𝐢𝐬 𝐭𝐨 𝐭𝐡𝐞 𝐰𝐨𝐫𝐥𝐝?” 🌍
